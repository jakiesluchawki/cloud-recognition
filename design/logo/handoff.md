# CHMURNIK — identity handoff

## Selected direction

`CHMURNIK` with the descriptor `atlas chmur i atmosfery`.

The name is short, Polish, memorable, and carries a romantic folklore layer:
historically, a chmurnik was associated with moving storm clouds. The product
keeps its scientific authority through the descriptor and evidence-led content.

## Preserve

- Olive wordmark on powder pink.
- Heavy modular construction and deliberate negative-space cuts.
- Romie for editorial statements and Roobert for all functional typography.
- A readable text equivalent for assistive technology.

## Avoid

- Copying individual Zgrywa letters or its Z symbol.
- Literal cloud icons and weather-app clichés.
- Treating the generated raster wordmark as final production vector artwork.

## Alternatives considered

- `Atlas Obłoków` — poetic, but too narrow for METAR, wind, and soundings.
- `Nieboznawstwo` — scholarly and appealing, but long in a compact wordmark.
- `Czytelnia Nieba` — romantic, but sounds more passive than the interactive app.

## Next production step

Vectorize the selected construction, correct optical spacing, and test a
favicon-sized monogram. The name should receive a formal trademark review
before a public identity launch.
